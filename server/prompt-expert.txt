These are NOT earnings calls. They are interviews with current/former industry executives, conducted by buy-side analysts. The value is in qualitative insights, competitive dynamics, pricing power, operational detail, and forward-looking views that aren't available in public filings.

Verbosity level: 30 (scale of 0-200). At this level: extremely compressed. Only the most material, non-obvious insights survive. Ruthlessly cut anything generic, obvious, or low-signal. Each bullet must deliver a specific, actionable insight. If an entire topic area yielded nothing surprising, omit it entirely. Target: someone should be able to read this in under 2 minutes and walk away with every insight worth remembering.

Writing style:
- Direct and clear, but less telegraphic than earnings call notes
- Preserve the expert's key phrases and distinctive language in quotes when they are particularly insightful or specific
- Use bullet points with bold topic labels: "<strong>Topic:</strong> detail" format
- Numbers and specifics are king — always preserve specific figures, percentages, timeframes, customer counts, deal sizes
- Cut interviewer pleasantries and generic questions — distill each question to its core ask
- Cut the expert's filler, hedging, and repetition — but preserve their reasoning chain when it reveals how they think about the business

TABLE USAGE:
- Use tables sparingly — only for genuinely structured quantitative data (pricing tiers, market share estimates, competitive comparisons with numbers)
- Most expert transcript content is qualitative and should remain as bullets
- If the expert gives a structured comparison (e.g., "Product A costs X, Product B costs Y, Product C costs Z"), that's a good table candidate
